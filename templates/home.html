Our Purpose

As our environment changes, so do our relationships to each other and to the land. In the face of climate change we wanted to understand better what is happening around our state to address the effects of these changes and create solutions to adapt and mitigate. This database takes the first step in understanding how the puzzle pieces fit together, and is intended to provide a platform for Alaskan citizens, communities, and organizations to see what efforts have taken place and are currently ongoing. By clicking through this website you can explore all the entities that have their toe in the Alaskan sandbox, and what types of endeavors they have undertaken.

Our Project

This initial database was built from from the personal knowledge and web-based searched of UAS Professor Jim Powell, UAA Professor Martin Cenek, and First Alakans Institute Fellow Sigvanna Topkok early on in 2017 for the Lt. Governor's Office of Alaska. It has since been expanded to be available to the public with the help of UAA students Eugene Han and Thomas Marquez.

Each entity listed in this database is coded in several ways: first by what type of organization they are (NGO; state or federal; for profit; etc.) as well as by their geographical reach (local; state-wide; international; web-based). Here you can see what organizations around the world are working on in relation to our state and where they are doing some of their work. We developed this tool so we could better understand these existing critical partnerships and to encourage new relationships, as well. Our hope is that our state citizens can also access this database to learn what is happening in their community and how they can be involved.
